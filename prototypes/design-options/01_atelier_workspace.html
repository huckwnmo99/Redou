Highlights linked to notes

The layered bed configuration delayed the onset of carbon dioxide penetration into the zeolite layer. This protection effect increased hydrogen purity stability during the adsorption step.

The authors also observed that recovery decreased at higher adsorption pressure, suggesting a trade-off that depends on cycle timing and feed composition.

Figure 4 connects the proposed barrier mechanism with the breakthrough shape. This paragraph is a good place for a method-related note.
