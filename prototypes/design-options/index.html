Neutral glass workspace

Ten directions with the same information architecture.

All ten concepts use a restrained, neutral palette with a soft glass layer. What changes is density, reading emphasis, and how strongly the app feels like a studio versus an editor.

01 Atelier Workspace
Balanced 3-panel layout. Warm stone glass. Good default candidate.
 02 Editorial Reader
Paper-like center reader with more calm and hierarchy for long reading.
 03 Slate Grid
Sharper grid and more structured cards. Slightly more technical.
 04 Detached Workspace
Shows a floating detached notes window as part of the design language.
 05 Dense Lab Board
Compact and information-rich. Better for heavy users with many papers.
06 Monograph Studio
More like a serious annotated manuscript than an app dashboard.
 07 Panorama Canvas
Wide panoramic layout with a ribbon-like command surface.
 08 Index Card Stack
Notion-adjacent card stack with softer modular blocks.
 09 Command Atlas
Operational and analytical, but still restrained and neutral.
 10 Archive Shelves
Feels closest to a personal research archive or curated library.
